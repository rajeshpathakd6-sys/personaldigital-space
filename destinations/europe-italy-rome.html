Getting to Know Rome

The Eternal City — layers of civilisation on every street corner. Whether you're here for a weekend or a full week, Rome rewards slow exploration. The best moments tend to happen not in the major landmarks but in the neighbourhoods between them — a morning market, a recommended local spot, a sunset you weren't expecting.

We visited Rome as part of our broader Italy journey and found it to be one of those places that sticks with you long after you leave. The food, the people, the rhythm of daily life — all of it leaves a mark.

Where to Eat

The food scene in Rome ranges from street stalls to destination restaurants. Our approach is always to eat where the locals eat — look for busy spots with no English menu outside and you're usually on the right track.

Getting Around

Most of Rome is navigable on foot or by a combination of local transport and occasional ride-hailing. We recommend getting a local SIM on arrival so you can use mapping apps freely.

Where to Stay

The best neighbourhoods to stay in Rome are generally those that put you close to the interesting areas without being in the tourist epicentre — you'll sleep better and feel more like a local.

Practical Tips

📅
Best Time to Visit Rome

Apr – Jun, Sep – Oct — this is when the weather is most favourable and crowds are manageable.

💱
Money

Currency is Euro (EUR). Carry some cash for local markets and small eateries — cards aren't always accepted.

🛂
Visa

Schengen visa / visa-free for EU & many. Always check the latest requirements before travelling.

📱
Connectivity

Get a local SIM on arrival — it's usually cheap and makes navigation, translation and booking infinitely easier.
