Our Story

It started with a missed flight
and a very long conversation

Back in 2022, we were the kind of people who planned travel down to the last museum opening hour. Then a strike grounded us in a small Portuguese town for two extra days — no plan, no agenda — and somewhere between a tiled courtyard and a third glass of local wine, we realised we'd been doing it all wrong.

Slowing down wasn't just pleasant. It was transformative. We started talking to strangers. We ate at places with hand-written menus and no reviews. We got lost in ways that led somewhere better than where we were headed. By the time we made it home, something had shifted.

"We didn't want to collect countries. We wanted to collect moments — the kind that still feel warm years later."

AdiRaj was born from that shift. Routes & Reflections — the name says it simply: we capture the journeys we take and the meaning we carry forward. It's our record of the slow routes — the hill towns and local markets, the unremarkable mornings that turned extraordinary, the food that made us call our parents to describe it. It's also where we share the practical bits: what worked, what didn't, and what we'd do differently.

Rajesh brings the planning instinct and the camera. Aditi brings the words and the willingness to try absolutely anything on a menu. Together, we make a reasonable team.

Our Philosophy

Slow. Curious. Authentic.

We're not chasing a list. We're chasing a feeling — the one you get when a place stops being a destination and starts feeling, briefly, like home. A few things guide how we travel and how we write:

01

Stay Longer, See Less

We'd rather spend a week in one place and actually understand it than tick five cities off in the same time. Depth over breadth, every time.

02

Eat Where the Locals Eat

The best meals have happened in places with plastic chairs and no English menu. We'll always pick a busy dhaba over a polished tourist restaurant.

03

Be Honest About the Hard Parts

Travel isn't always beautiful. We write about the exhaustion, the miscommunications, and the days that didn't go to plan — because that's part of it too.

04

Leave Things as We Found Them

We try to be thoughtful visitors — supporting local businesses, respecting communities, and travelling in a way we feel good about.
